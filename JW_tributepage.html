A little about John Williams

John Williams has composed many iconic film scores, such as Harry Potter, Star Wars, Close Encounters of the Third Kind, and JAWS to name a few. After studying at Jullilard and the Eastman School of Music he returned to Los Angeles where he began working as an orchestrator at film studios. Williams has composed at least 124 film scores, and has been nominated for 193 awards! Out of those he has won 5 Academy Awards, 3 Emmy Awards, 4 Golden Globes, 25 Grammy Awards, 7 Bristish Academy Film Awards, and 8 Saturn Awards.

Williams got his knack for music from his father, Johnny Williams, a jazz drummer and percussionist who played with the Raymond Scott Quintet. Outside of Hollywood, Williams served as the Boston Pops' conductor from 1980 to 1993 and also orchestrated theme music for the 1984 Summer Olympic Games and NBC Sunday Night Football.

Williams has three children with his late wife Barbara Ruick, and is currently married to Samantha Winslow.

A timeline of John Williams film scores and accomplishments

  • 1973: Tom Sawyer nominated for Academy Award & Golden Globe
  • 1975: Jaws wins Academy Award & Golden Globe for Best Original Score
  • 1977: Star Wars: Episode IV - A New Hope wins Academy Award & Golden Globe for Best Original Score
  • 1978: Superman wins Grammy Awards for Best Instrumental Composition and Best Album of Original Score Written for a Motion Picture
  • 1980: Star Wars: Episode V - The Empire Strikes Back nominated for Academy Award & Golden Globe
  • 1981: Indiana Jones and Raiders of the Lost Ark wins Grammy Award for Best Album of Original Score Written for a Motion Picture
  • 1982: E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial wins Academy Award & Golden Globe for Best Original Score
  • 1983: Star Wars: Episode VI - Return of the Jedi nominated for Academy Award & Golden Globe
  • 1990: Home Alone nominated for Academy Award & Grammy Award
  • 1993: Jurassic Park nominated for a Grammy Award
  • 1998: Saving Private Ryan wins Grammy Award for Best Instrumental Composition Written for a Motion Picture
  • 1999: Star Wars: Episode I - The Phantom Menance nominated for Grammy Award
  • 2001: Harry Potter and the Socerer's Stone nominated for Academy Award & Grammy Award
  • The list goes on and on!
