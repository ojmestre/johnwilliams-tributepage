A little about John Williams

John Williams has composed many iconic film scores, such as Harry Potter, Star Wars, and JAWS to name a few. After studying at Jullilard and the Eastman School of Music he returned to Los Angeles where he began working as an orchestrator at film studios.

A timeline of John Williams film scores and accomplishments

  • February 8, 1932: John Williams is born!
  • June 18, 1950: John Williams graduates from High School and attends University of California.
  • December 18, 1952: John Williams is drafted into the US Air Force where he conducted and arranged music for the US Air Force band.
  • August 17, 1956: John Williams marries Barbara Ruick.
  • 1971: John Williams composes Fiddler on the roof.
  • 1975: John Williams composes Jaws!
  • 1975: John Williams composes Star Wars.
  • December 18, 1975: John Williams receives his second Academy Award (Oscar) for his music featured in Jaws!
  • 1977: John Williams composes Close Encounters of the Third Kind.
  • December 18, 1977: John Williams receives his third Academy Award (Oscar) for his music featured in Star Wars!
  • 1978: John Williams composes Superman.
  • 1981: John Williams composes E.T.
  • 1981: John Williams composes Raiders of the Lost Ark.
  • 1993: John Williams composes Schindler's List.
  • 2001: John Williams composes Harry Potter.
